May 30, 2025

1. $AVST.L – Avast PLC
Cybersecurity player positioned at the intersection of consumer protection and enterprise expansion.

Why I’m watching: As digital threats surge, demand for endpoint protection and antivirus tools is only intensifying. Avast's renewed enterprise focus and M&A potential make it a sleeper growth candidate.

Sentiment: Quietly accumulating — not flashy, but respected among value-oriented tech watchers.

My take: A steady climber with defensive tech appeal. I’m watching for institutional inflows and relative strength against FTSE tech peers.

2. $HL.L – Hargreaves Lansdown
UK’s go-to platform for retail investing — part broker, part fintech infrastructure.

Why I’m watching: With retail engagement cyclical, a rebound in investor activity post-rate cuts could boost volumes. Tech platform updates add upside optionality.

Sentiment: Watchful — sentiment mixed, but long-term faith from funds remains intact.

My take: If sentiment in UK equities improves, HL is a clean beta play on domestic investor participation. I’m watching for trend reversals and rising user growth.

3. $SGE.L – Sage Group
Cloud accounting and enterprise resource software for SMBs, with growing AI-enhanced features.

Why I’m watching: Consistent margins, reliable SaaS revenues, and quietly increasing automation exposure make this a low-volatility compounder.

Sentiment: Understated — favored by long-term growth portfolios but not in speculative circles.

My take: Steady trend higher. I’m watching earnings for acceleration. This is the kind of stock that rewards patience, not hype.

4. $ITV.L – ITV PLC
Legacy media firm making an aggressive pivot to digital with ITVX streaming platform.

Why I’m watching: Valuation is depressed, but ad spend recovery and platform monetization may surprise. Strong local brand recognition gives a strategic edge.

Sentiment: Speculative contrarian — unloved, but some bottom-fishing visible from hedge funds.

My take: Pure contrarian play. I’m watching volume spikes for signals that narrative is shifting.

5. $EXPN.L – Experian PLC
Global leader in credit reporting and data analytics, quietly powering the backbone of financial decision-making.

Why I’m watching: As credit markets normalise and lenders seek smarter risk models, Experian’s data-driven services are becoming mission-critical. Its B2B demand is resilient, and expansion into emerging markets supports long-term growth.

Sentiment: Viewed as defensive tech — popular among institutional allocators seeking growth with downside insulation.

My take: Technically coiled, fundamentally robust. If macro volatility recedes and credit origination picks up, Experian could re-rate meaningfully. I’m watching for a breakout above recent highs to initiate exposure.
