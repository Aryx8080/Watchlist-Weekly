Watchlist Weekly

by Aryan Chandra

A curated weekly stock watchlist built on trends, momentum, and sentiment. Updated every Sunday.

$AVST.L – Avast PLC

A mature cybersecurity provider with a growing footprint in enterprise SaaS.

Why I’m watching: Consistent margin performance, increasing adoption across regulated sectors, and a strategic pivot toward AI-enhanced threat analytics position Avast as an overlooked compounder in the defensive tech space.

Sentiment: Institutional quiet buy — strong fundamentals, low hype.

My take: Accumulate on weakness. The valuation is still attractive relative to peers, especially given its free cash flow profile and potential for consolidation.

$BARC.L – Barclays PLC

Large-cap UK bank with international exposure and undervalued multiples.

Why I’m watching: Potential rate cut tailwinds from BoE, low P/E vs global peers, plus dividend yield keeps it attractive to income-focused investors.

Sentiment: Value-oriented — still unloved but not ignored.

My take: I’m in if macro stabilizes. Rotation into financials may finally stick this time.

$PTH.L – PureTech Health

A life sciences R&D incubator blending biotech and data-driven therapeutics.

Why I’m watching: Diversified pipeline, equity stakes in spinouts, and strong academic partnerships make this a strategic exposure to next-gen healthcare innovation.

Sentiment: Niche speculative — volatile, but unique IP portfolio.

My take: Not for the faint-hearted. Long-term asymmetric play if biotech markets thaw.

$EXPN.L – Experian PLC

Consumer credit data behemoth quietly transforming with AI-driven analytics.

Why I’m watching: Credit cycle tailwinds and digitization of financial services put Experian at the heart of decision-making infrastructure. Sticky recurring revenues and global footprint.

Sentiment: Quietly confident — favored by long-term capital and consistently included in quality-focused portfolios.

My take: Resilient through cycles. I see this as a core portfolio asset, especially for defensive exposure in an otherwise volatile tech-finance crossover.

$LMT – Lockheed Martin

Global leader in aerospace and defense, specializing in advanced military technology and space systems.

Why I’m watching: Lockheed Martin is a cornerstone of national security, with stable government contracts and cutting-edge technology investments in hypersonics and AI-driven defense solutions.

Sentiment: Defensive — strong revenue streams backed by government spending.

My take: LMT is a long-term stability play. While valuation is on the higher side, defense spending trends and its innovation pipeline make it a compelling watch.
